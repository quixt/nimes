The Festival de Nîmes is a summer music festival held in the French city of Nîmes. The festival was founded in 1981 and takes place every year in July. The festival features a wide range of music genres, including rock, pop, electronic, and hip-hop. past performers include Muse, The Libertines, and Snoop Dogg. The festival takes place in the city's Roman amphitheatre, which was built in the 1st century AD.

Nimes, in the south of France, is a city with a rich history dating back to the Roman Empire. Every year, the city celebrates the Pentecost Feria, a festival that commemorates the descent of the Holy Spirit upon the disciples of Jesus Christ.
The festival, which takes place over five days, features a number of traditional French activities, including bullfights, concerts, and parades. On the final day of the festival, a large procession makes its way through the city, culminating in a mass at the cathedral.
The Pentecost Feria is a cherished tradition in Nimes, and one that attracts visitors from all over the world. If you find yourself in France during the Pentecost season, be sure to check out this unique festival!

The Games are a modern adaptation of the ancient Roman festival, the Ludi Romani. The Games include chariot races, foot races, and javelin competitions, as well as gladiator fights.
The chariot races are held in the arena at the Nimes Amphitheatre, which is a replica of the Roman Colosseum. The races are between teams of two horses and two chariots. There are eight teams in total, and each team races three times.
The foot races are held on a track in the arena. The races are between teams of four runners. There are eight teams in total, and each team races three times.
The javelin competition is held in the arena. The competition is between teams of two javelin throwers. There are eight teams in total, and each team competes three times.
The gladiator fights are held in the arena. The fights are between teams of two gladiators. There are eight teams in total, and each team fights three times.
